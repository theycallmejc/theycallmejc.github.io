How I Think About Systems

First principles over cargo-culting patterns.

Design for Failure First

I assume every component will fail. That means circuit breakers, dead-letter queues, retry budgets, and graceful degradation are non-negotiable — not optional add-ons. Systems that look perfect under normal load are the ones that page you hardest at 3 AM.

Chaos EngineeringFMEADLQRetry Budgets

Observability ≠ Monitoring

Metrics, logs, and traces are table stakes. True observability means you can answer questions you haven't thought of yet — without deploying new instrumentation. I build systems where unknown unknowns surface naturally.

Distributed TracingSLO/SLI/SLAOpenTelemetry

Platform as a Product

Platform teams exist to reduce cognitive load on application developers. The measure of success isn't infrastructure reliability — it's developer velocity. Golden paths, self-service, and internal docs are engineering deliverables, not nice-to-haves.

IDPDeveloper ExperienceBackstage

Cost is a Feature

Every architecture decision has a cost dimension. I factor in reserved vs on-demand, S3 storage classes, Lambda cold starts vs provisioned concurrency, and Spot Instance viability during design — not after the AWS bill arrives.
